What This Class Is

You will take apart a real engine, perform real vehicle maintenance, and build a complete guide to owning a car—one you can actually use after high school. Half your time is hands-on in the shop. The other half is building a portfolio of research that covers everything from buying your first car to keeping it running.

Module Breakdown

Module 1 — Before You Touch Anything

Safety quizzes for every piece of shop equipment. Must pass General Shop Safety before unlocking anything else.

Module 2 — Tear It Down, Learn How It Works

Full disassembly and reassembly of a Predator 212cc engine. 24 photos, 3 videos, 10 written assessments, 6 instructor checkpoints.

100 points • Teacher-graded

Module 3 — Hands On: Real Maintenance, Real Tools

Oil Change Lab (50 pts) — Demo on Mr. McAteer’s car first, then full oil change on your own vehicle. Document both phases.
Brake Check & Rotor Turning Lab (75 pts) — Demo inspection + measurements on Mr. McAteer’s car, then independent brake inspection on your own vehicle. Lathe work is teacher-supervised.

Both labs are teacher-graded. You submit demo notes, your vehicle’s specs, photo documentation, and a write-up.

Module 4 — What Nobody Teaches You About Owning a Car

16 research assignments that build a complete vehicle ownership portfolio. You pick one car at the start and carry it through every assignment. Topics cover everything you need to know before buying, financing, insuring, maintaining, and eventually selling a vehicle.
