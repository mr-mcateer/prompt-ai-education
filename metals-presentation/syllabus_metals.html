What This Class Is

You will learn to identify metals, read blueprints, operate shop equipment safely, and build real projects from raw material. The semester starts with safety certifications and material science, then moves into progressively complex fabrication projects—from CAD modeling to sheet metal to welding—and finishes with a capstone where you design, build, price, and sell a product you made.

Module Breakdown

Module 1 — Shop Safety Certifications

You must pass General Shop Safety before touching any equipment. Then complete certifications for each machine: angle grinder, cutoff saw, arc welder, and bandsaw.

Sequential unlock — pass each quiz to access the next machine • Citizenship & Safety grade

Module 2 — Know Your Metals

Study guide covering ferrous vs. non-ferrous metals, 9 metal types, identification methods, and key properties. Unit quiz: 25 multiple-choice questions, 30-minute time limit, 2 attempts.

25 points • Citizenship & Safety grade

Module 3 — Design & Build

Four progressive projects, each building new skills:

Project Pts Skills
Portable Tool Organizer — SolidWorks 30 CAD modeling, parametric design, file management
Sheet Metal Cell Phone Stand — POP Writeup 50 Plan of Procedure, sheet metal layout, bending, assembly
Campus Handrail Plaque — Design & Fabrication 70 Design for permanent install, cutting, welding, finishing
Plasma Cutter Practice Lab 20 Plasma cutting technique, safety, straight/curved cuts

All projects are teacher-graded with detailed rubrics.

Module 4 — Metal Entrepreneur Challenge

Capstone project: identify a real need, design a metal product to fill it, build it to professional quality, price it with a full cost breakdown, and sell it to a real customer. This is where you put everything together.

100 points • Teacher-graded

Module 5 — Shop Citizenship

Every Thursday, last 15 minutes of class: full shop cleanup. Tools returned, stations wiped, floor swept, scrap sorted. 10 points each week — graded on effort, quality, and attitude. This is part of your Citizenship & Safety grade (25% of your overall grade).

Deep Clean Day — Monday 3/30 (first day back from spring break): Full-period, top-to-bottom shop restoration. Every surface, every machine, every drawer, every corner. 20 points.

18 weekly cleanups + 1 deep clean = 200 points total in this module.
